03 MEASUREMENT FORMAT

7 days of monitoring
under working load

The check runs in the facility's working mode, to see how the network behaves over time, not only its state at the moment of inspection.

01

Under working load

The network is recorded in real operating conditions.

02

Across different periods

Measurements show how parameters change over the week.

03

With events logged

The report captures significant deviations and when they occur.

04

With a technical conclusion

The result is presented clearly and is ready for further decisions about the facility.

We reference IEC 61000-4-30, IEC 61000-4-7 and EN 50160. The list of measured parameters depends on the task and the facility's operating mode.

04 NETWORK PARAMETERS

Power quality parameters

We analyse the groups of parameters that show the network's operating mode, load, deviations and events over time.

01

Voltage and current

Show the basic supply mode and the actual load on the network.

02

Phase imbalance

Helps assess how evenly load is distributed across a three-phase system.

03

Voltage dips and swells

May be linked to unstable equipment operation, automation or the connection of loads.

04

Harmonic distortion

Often arises with inverters, variable-frequency drives, UPS units and other non-linear loads.

05

Reactive power

Shows excess load on network components and how efficiently power is used.

06

Events over time

Reveal when deviations occur and which operating mode they relate to.

05 DELIVERABLE

What you receive

The check ends with a technical report. It records the events, shows the parameter graphs and states a conclusion that decisions can rest on.

01

Network events

What was recorded and at which moments of operation it occurred.

02

Parameter graphs

The behaviour of voltage, current and other parameters over the measurement period.

03

Comparison against standards

Deviations are assessed with reference to EN 50160.

04

Technical conclusion

What it means in practice and which questions are worth considering next.

Measurements are taken by a power quality analyser installed at the measurement point for the whole period of the check.

06 APPROACH

Data first,
conclusions after

PowerTech works with tasks where inspecting the switchboard or hearing a description isn't enough. What matters to us is separating facts from assumptions and showing what is happening with the power supply in the facility's real operation.

The result is formulated as a technical conclusion: what was recorded, how significant it is and which questions are worth considering next.

Our task is not to replace the designer or contractor, but to provide a measured technical picture for decision-making.
