03 MEASUREMENT FORMAT

7 days of monitoring
during normal operation

Monitoring is carried out while the facility is operating normally, so the electrical system can be assessed over time rather than at a single point during an inspection.

01

During normal operation

Measurements are recorded under actual operating conditions.

02

Across different operating periods

The data show how parameters vary over the week.

03

With events logged

Significant power quality events and their timing are recorded in the report.

04

With an engineering assessment

The findings are presented clearly to support informed decisions about the facility.

Measurement methods and interpretation are based on IEC 61000-4-30; harmonic and interharmonic measurements are based on IEC 61000-4-7. Where measurements are made at the point of supply and EN 50160 is applicable, supply-voltage characteristics are compared with the values specified in that standard. The parameters included depend on the survey objective and the facility's operating conditions.

04 MEASURED PARAMETERS
01

Voltage and current

Show voltage conditions and actual current loading at the measurement point.

02

Voltage unbalance

Quantifies asymmetry within a three-phase voltage system. Possible contributing factors include uneven phase loading and upstream supply asymmetry; voltage unbalance can adversely affect three-phase equipment.

03

Voltage dips and swells

Dips may occur during network faults or motor starting; swells may occur after load rejection or switching operations. Both can cause equipment trips, process interruptions or control-system malfunctions.

04

Harmonic distortion

Commonly associated with inverters, variable-frequency drives, UPS systems and other non-linear loads.

05

Reactive power

Together with active and apparent power, it helps assess power factor and the current loading and losses associated with reactive power flow.

06

Events over time

Show when deviations occur and help correlate them with the facility's operating conditions.

05 DELIVERABLE
01

Power quality events

Events recorded during operation and their timing.

02

Parameter graphs

Trends in voltage, current and other parameters over the monitoring period.

03

Assessment against applicable criteria

Where measurements are made at the point of supply and EN 50160 is applicable, voltage characteristics are compared with the values specified in that standard.

04

Engineering assessment

What the findings mean in practice and what should be investigated next.

Measurements are made using a power quality analyser installed at the measurement point for the full monitoring period.

06 APPROACH

Data first,
conclusions second

PowerTech works on cases where a switchboard inspection or a verbal description is not enough. We separate measured facts from assumptions and show how the electrical system behaves while the facility is operating.

The findings are presented as an engineering assessment: what was recorded, how significant it is and what should be investigated next.

Our role is not to replace the designer or contractor, but to provide a measured, factual basis for decision-making.
